1. Scope and Reading Guide

This Privacy Policy explains the data-boundary model for TinyHumanMD. It applies to pages and features served from this site. It does not govern independent third-party sites, even if linked, and does not replace your institution’s internal privacy program.

The policy is written for healthcare-adjacent usage where legal risk can arise from assumptions. Its purpose is to define boundaries clearly: what this site is, what it is not, and where responsibility stays with users and institutions.

Users should read this policy together with the Terms of Use because both documents form a single risk-allocation framework.

2. Service Characterization

TinyHumanMD is a decision-support reference interface. It is not an EHR, not an EMR, not a billing platform, not a patient messaging channel, and not a treatment-delivery service. The site is designed to aid structured thinking, not to assume custodial control of patient operations.

Because of this characterization, users must not infer that site availability or content language creates enterprise-grade compliance commitments. If your workflow needs certified controls, contractual assurances, or audited retention systems, you must obtain those separately.

This statement applies to the maximum extent permitted by law and is intended to prevent category confusion between informational tools and regulated systems.

3. Definitions

For this policy, “user” means any person or organization accessing the site. “Institution” means the organization for which the user acts. “Personal information” has the meaning given by applicable law in your jurisdiction. “PHI” refers to protected health information where healthcare privacy law applies.

“Local environment” means your own browser, device, network, and institution-managed infrastructure. “Third-party infrastructure” means services not operated by the site owner, including providers used for delivery, networking, or ancillary functions.

Definitions are interpretive tools and should not be used to imply obligations beyond explicit policy text.

4. Responsibility for User Inputs

You are solely responsible for what you enter, copy, export, interpret, and operationalize. If you enter sensitive information, you must have legal authority and policy authorization to do so. Unauthorized entry is a user-side compliance failure.

TinyHumanMD cannot evaluate whether your specific data is lawfully processable in your jurisdiction or institution. You must conduct your own legal and operational review.

Where local policy prohibits non-approved tools for specific data categories, local policy controls and you must not use this site for that workflow.

5. Local Device and Endpoint Controls

Endpoint security is your responsibility. You must manage authentication, session hygiene, physical access control, network security, and local artifact handling on devices used to access TinyHumanMD.

Risks from shared devices, screen exposure, insecure Wi-Fi, stale sessions, and unmanaged browser profiles are user-owned risks. The site owner is not responsible for endpoint misconfiguration.

Institutions should apply their own hardening baselines and training controls before permitting operational use.

6. Data Minimization Position

TinyHumanMD is designed around minimal mandatory data collection for access. The site is not intended as a repository for full patient records or long-term regulated storage.

You should enter only what is necessary for lawful use and should avoid including direct identifiers unless authorized and required by your governance model.

Data minimization is a user and institution behavior requirement as much as a product-design objective.

7. Third-Party Boundaries

The website may rely on third-party infrastructure and may link to third-party resources. Those services are controlled by their operators and governed by separate terms and privacy notices.

You are responsible for evaluating whether third-party dependencies satisfy your procurement and compliance standards. TinyHumanMD does not guarantee that third-party services meet your specific legal or institutional requirements.

External links are informational and do not constitute endorsement or legal representation.

8. Security Disclaimer and Residual Risk

Reasonable security measures may be applied, but no internet-delivered service guarantees absolute confidentiality, integrity, or availability. Residual risk is inherent in networked systems.

Users accept residual risk from provider incidents, route instability, endpoint compromise, and human error. Institutions remain responsible for incident triage, escalation, and statutory notifications where required.

This section does not waive non-waivable rights under local law but limits implied guarantees to the maximum extent legally permitted.

9. Regulatory Position and HIPAA/BAA Limits

TinyHumanMD is not represented as a HIPAA covered-entity service, Business Associate service, or certified health-record platform. Site access alone does not create a BAA or equivalent regulated processing contract.

If your workflow requires such contractual controls, you must establish them through separate legal channels and approved vendors. Do not infer those controls from public website availability.

Regulatory responsibility remains with user institutions implementing outputs in clinical or operational contexts.

10. US State Rights and UK/EU Overlay

Where US state privacy laws grant rights (for example access, deletion, correction, or appeal in specified contexts), requests may be submitted through the published contact channel and will be handled as required by applicable law to the extent relevant information is held and verifiable.

For UK/EU users, rights under applicable data-protection frameworks may include access, rectification, erasure, restriction, objection, and complaint rights, subject to lawful limitations and verification requirements.

These overlays are informational summaries and not legal advice. Users should consult counsel for jurisdiction-specific implementation details.

11. Cross-Border Access

If you access TinyHumanMD from outside your primary jurisdiction, you are responsible for complying with local law, including data-transfer restrictions, professional licensing limits, and institutional governance requirements.

No representation is made that site use is appropriate or lawful in every jurisdiction. Access where prohibited is unauthorized.

Cross-border compliance review is an institutional obligation and cannot be outsourced to this policy text.

12. Retention, Deletion, and Audit Expectations

Where data exists in local environments, users and institutions control retention and deletion through their own systems and policies. TinyHumanMD does not replace record-retention governance obligations.

Institutions should define clear retention schedules, deletion standards, and audit controls for any workflow that references site outputs.

If your compliance framework requires immutable logs or regulated retention attestation, implement those controls independently.

13. Policy Changes and Versioning

This policy may be updated at any time. Updated versions are effective when posted with a revised date. Continued site use after posting indicates acceptance of revised language.

If your institution needs fixed-policy baselines, you must maintain your own document snapshots and change control process rather than rely on real-time public text alone.

Version management is particularly important for high-risk contexts where interpretation drift can create operational exposure.

14. Final Responsibility Statement

TinyHumanMD provides informational support only. Final accountability for legal compliance, privacy governance, clinical safety review, and implementation outcomes remains with users and their institutions.

If your use case requires guaranteed regulatory compliance, contractual service levels, or certified control frameworks, you must use systems designed and contracted for that purpose.

By using this site, you acknowledge and accept this allocation model.
