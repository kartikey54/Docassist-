Privacy Policy

Effective date: February 12, 2026

Last updated: February 12, 2026

Privacy risk in healthcare-adjacent systems is frequently created by assumptions. This policy is designed to remove those assumptions and define practical responsibility boundaries. TinyHumanMD is a decision-support reference tool and not a substitute for institutional governance, legal review, security controls, or licensed clinical judgment.

Users and institutions remain responsible for lawful operation, data-handling choices, and final implementation decisions. This policy should be read with the Terms of Use and Medical Disclaimer; both documents form one boundary framework.

1. Purpose and Legal Framing

Section 1 explains the privacy boundary document for a clinical decision-support website. This section exists to prevent ambiguity and to ensure that website use is interpreted as informational support rather than delegated compliance or treatment responsibility.

A recurring failure mode addressed in Section 1 is category confusion between public informational software and regulated healthcare platforms. If this risk is left unmanaged, downstream legal and operational exposure increases regardless of website wording or interface design.

Section 1 therefore places primary accountability on users and institutions for review, authorization, and implementation readiness. A recommended baseline control is formal internal tool-approval workflow, supported by documented ownership and supervisory enforcement.

Where local law creates non-waivable rights, those rights continue to apply. Where contractual allocation is permitted, Section 1 allocates implementation and compliance risk to the user side rather than the site operator.
